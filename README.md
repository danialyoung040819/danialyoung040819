## 🧪 Research Background

### Ir-Catalyzed C–C Bond Formation

I previously worked on an organometallic catalysis project involving **Ir-catalyzed C–C bond formation between benzyl alcohols and alkyl sulfones**. This project helped me build a strong foundation in reaction design, condition optimization, mechanistic analysis, and air-sensitive synthesis.

Skills developed from this project include:

- Inert-atmosphere synthesis
- Organometallic catalyst preparation
- Reaction optimization
- TLC monitoring
- GC–MS analysis
- NMR characterization
- Mechanistic interpretation

This experience strengthened my understanding of how catalyst structure, base selection, solvent effects, and reaction temperature can influence selectivity and product formation.

### Fluorinated Ylide Monomers and Polymerization

My later research shifted toward **fluorinated sulfonium ylide monomers** and their polymerization behavior. This work connects organic synthesis with polymer/materials chemistry and energy-storage applications.

Key themes include:

- Multi-step monomer synthesis
- Fluorinated molecular design
- Radical polymerization screening
- Copolymerization attempts
- Preliminary controlled polymerization exploration
- Troubleshooting low conversion, gelation, and stability issues
- Connecting monomer structure to polymerization behavior

This project shaped my current interest in designing functional polymers for battery-relevant applications, including polymer electrolytes, electrode binders, coatings, and interfacial layers.
